# Ranking and Output

## Decision Order

1. Apply confirmed hard filters.
2. Separate options blocked by decisive unknowns.
3. Assess application feasibility.
4. Compare recurring and move-in cost.
5. Compare every important commute.
6. Compare property and lifestyle fit.
7. Adjust recommendation for availability, evidence confidence, and risk.

Do not let luxury amenities erase a hard failure.

## Recommendation Bands

- **Strong match**: passes hard constraints, good commute/cost/fit, no major unresolved issue.
- **Worth pursuing**: passes hard constraints with a clear, acceptable compromise.
- **Verify first**: could be a good match, but one decisive fact is unknown.
- **Backup/watch**: viable only if the market stays tight or a preference relaxes.
- **Exclude**: confirmed hard failure, ineligibility, or unacceptable risk.

If a numeric model helps a large comparison, show weights and inputs, keep unknowns separate, and avoid decimals that imply unsupported precision.

## Multi-commute Comparison

Show:

- each essential destination separately;
- weighted typical commute;
- worst essential commute;
- route complexity and alternative routes.

Never hide an unacceptable destination inside an average.

## Shortlist Table

Use compact columns:

| Option | Rent / est. total | Move date | Property | Commutes | Key fit | Compromise / unknown | Confidence | Action |
|---|---:|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Link directly to the unit. Identify duplicate adverts and multiple units in the same building.

## Decision Summary

Lead with:

1. best options and why;
2. the binding constraint;
3. important compromises;
4. what changed from the user's original assumptions, if anything.

End with:

- which listings to contact first;
- exact questions for each;
- application-readiness categories;
- pre-signing/pre-payment checks.

When no good match exists, keep comparables separate and propose the smallest relaxation: a modest budget step, a nearby area, a slightly longer commute, a flexible date, or a lower-priority amenity. Never relax a constraint without saying so.

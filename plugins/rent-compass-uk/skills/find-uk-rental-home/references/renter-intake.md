# Renter Intake

## Intake Strategy

Gather information progressively. Do not present a long questionnaire when five answers are enough to begin.

For a live search, prioritize:

1. nation/city or commute destination;
2. move-in window and intended duration;
3. ideal rent and absolute maximum;
4. household and minimum bedrooms/bathrooms;
5. commute ceiling and allowed modes.

Ask follow-ups only when the answer changes geography, eligibility, hard filters, or material cost.

## Profile Schema

### Household

- number of adults and children;
- student, employed, self-employed/contractor, retired, new arrival, or mixed household;
- couple, family, individual, or unrelated sharers;
- pets;
- accessibility needs;
- whether a guarantor is available or rent in advance is feasible, if relevant.

Do not assume that a student wants student accommodation or that an employed renter wants a city-centre flat.

### Dates

- earliest and latest move-in;
- fixed deadline or flexible target;
- expected duration;
- whether temporary accommodation can bridge a gap.

### Budget

Record separately:

- ideal advertised rent;
- absolute rent ceiling;
- ideal total monthly housing cost;
- absolute total monthly ceiling;
- maximum acceptable move-in cash requirement, if the user wants to state it;
- whether council tax/rates and utilities must be included.

Never count a stretch budget as the default.

### Property

- whole property, room, house share, build-to-rent, or student accommodation;
- minimum bedrooms and bathrooms;
- furnished, part-furnished, or unfurnished;
- required versus preferred floor, lift, parking, outdoor space, storage, appliances, concierge, gym, workspace, parcel handling, or other amenities;
- acceptable property age or condition;
- smoking, pet, or accessibility constraints.

### Location and Lifestyle

- commute destinations and schedule;
- shops, healthcare, schools, green space, nightlife, community, airport or rail access;
- quiet versus lively environment;
- tolerance for road, rail, nightlife, construction, or aircraft noise;
- city-centre access;
- areas or buildings explicitly excluded.

Avoid demographic proxies when discussing neighbourhood fit.

## Requirement Classes

Label every stated preference:

- **Hard**: must hold.
- **Strong**: important compromise.
- **Nice**: tie-breaker.
- **Unknown**: clarification may be needed.

When requirements conflict, name the conflict and ask the user which dimension can flex. Do not silently downgrade a hard constraint.

## Applicant Categories

Use renter category only to prompt relevant checks:

- Students: accommodation type, guarantor/upfront terms, council tax/rates position, academic dates, dual occupancy.
- Employed renters: affordability evidence, probation/new job, commute schedule.
- Self-employed/contractors: evidence period and alternative referencing routes.
- New arrivals: local credit history, Right to Rent in England, remote viewing and payment risk.
- Families: bedroom needs, children-related constraints, schools only when requested.
- Sharers: joint versus individual liability, HMO/licensing checks, replacement-tenant terms.
- Pet owners: written pet policy and building/lease restrictions.
- Accessibility needs: step-free route, lift reliability, entrances, internal layout, parking, and verified measurements.

Describe likely checks; never declare an applicant eligible without the provider's criteria.

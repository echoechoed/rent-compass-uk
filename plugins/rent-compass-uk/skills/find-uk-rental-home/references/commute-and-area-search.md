# Commute and Area Search

## Commute Anchor Schema

For each destination capture:

- exact address, building, campus, station, or postcode;
- days per week or month;
- desired arrival/departure time;
- allowed modes: walk, cycle, bus, tram, underground, train, drive;
- maximum door-to-door time;
- maximum walking and interchange tolerance;
- importance: essential, frequent, or occasional.

Resolve ambiguous destination names before relying on them.

## Candidate Area Method

1. Identify viable corridors and interchange points for each essential destination.
2. Intersect the useful catchments for multiple frequent destinations.
3. Add areas that offer a small rent or property-quality advantage for a modest commute trade-off.
4. Search by area, station catchment, postcode, and named building.
5. Check the exact listing address or nearest disclosed location; do not use an area-centre journey as the unit's commute.

Use current journey planners or transport operators. Straight-line distance is only a discovery hint.

## Time Assumptions

Prefer door-to-door estimates that include:

- walking to and from stops;
- typical wait/interchange time;
- scheduled travel time;
- parking or station access where relevant.

Check the user's actual commute window when practical. Label estimates such as “weekday 08:30 arrival” or “off-peak”. Flag routes with:

- a single fragile connection;
- infrequent service;
- last-mile difficulty;
- material peak/off-peak variation;
- planned disruption found in current sources.

Do not claim a guaranteed journey time.

## Multiple Destinations

Evaluate three views:

- **Weighted typical commute**: frequency × realistic journey time.
- **Worst essential commute**: the hardest destination that still matters.
- **Reliability/complexity**: number of changes and alternative routes.

Do not average away an unacceptable essential commute.

## Area Fit

Separate observable evidence from judgement:

- access: routes, frequencies, walking, cycling, roads;
- daily life: shops, healthcare, schools if requested, parks and services;
- environment: disclosed construction, main roads, rail lines, nightlife;
- safety: current official statistics or clearly scoped sources only.

Do not label an area safe or unsafe from reputation, property price, ethnicity, student share, or deprivation alone. Explain the geographic and temporal limits of any crime data.

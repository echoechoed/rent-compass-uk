# Listing Verification

## Unit Record

Normalize each listing into:

- direct URL, platform, advertiser, listing ID if shown;
- added/reduced/access date;
- full or partial address and building;
- rent amount and frequency;
- deposit, holding payment, rent in advance, and disclosed fees;
- availability and tenancy/contract term;
- property type, bedrooms, bathrooms, furnishing;
- floor, lift, parking, outdoor space, and amenities;
- included/excluded bills;
- applicant, household, pet, age, or occupancy restrictions;
- EPC or local equivalent when applicable;
- commute evidence;
- decisive unknowns.

Do not copy facts from another unit in the building.

## Evidence Labels

- **Verified**: current, appropriate source directly supports the claim.
- **Advertised**: only the advertiser states it.
- **Inferred**: indirect evidence supports a reasoned conclusion.
- **Unknown**: absent, inconsistent, or too stale.

Attach confidence to the claim, not to the listing as a whole.

## Identity and Authority Checks

Where applicable and possible:

- compare advertiser name, phone/domain, and payment instructions;
- verify agent redress/client-money claims from the relevant official register;
- check landlord/agent registration where the nation provides a register;
- check HMO or selective/additional licensing with the local authority when household setup makes it relevant;
- verify the building and address exist independently;
- compare photos, floor plan, rent and description across duplicates;
- verify who is entitled to receive money before the user pays.

Absence from a searchable register may have exemptions or data lag. Report it as unresolved, not automatic fraud.

## Scam and Quality Signals

Escalate for verification when several signals appear:

- pressure to pay before viewing, identity checks, or written terms;
- payment to an unrelated person, crypto, gift card, or unusual overseas account;
- price materially below comparable homes without explanation;
- copied photos, inconsistent address, or impossible floor plan;
- refusal to identify the landlord/agent or explain deposit protection;
- advert and contract/payment details do not match;
- remote-only process with no credible live viewing or representative;
- urgency used to prevent ordinary checks.

One signal is not proof. Explain the evidence and the safe next check.

## Viewing Preparation

Generate listing-specific questions about decisive unknowns. Include physical checks where relevant:

- damp, mould, leaks, heating, glazing and ventilation;
- water pressure, appliances, storage and mobile/internet coverage;
- noise with windows open and closed;
- entrances, locks, communal areas, bins and parcel arrangements;
- exact furniture and inventory;
- fire/CO alarms and safety documents;
- promised repairs and the date they will be completed.

Ask for important promises to be written into the agreement or documented by the provider.

# England Route

Verified against official sources on 2026-07-23. Recheck at answer time.

## Routing Notes

- Use this file only for property in England.
- Distinguish mainstream private assured tenancies from halls, lodgers, social housing, holiday accommodation, and other excluded arrangements.
- Since 1 May 2026, the Renters' Rights Act changes apply to private renting in England. Most existing assured shorthold tenancies became assured periodic tenancies, and new mainstream private tenancies are generally assured periodic tenancies.
- Do not rely on pre-May-2026 fixed-term/Section 21 guidance for a current mainstream private tenancy without checking transitional rules.

## Checks Before Commitment

- Verify the current tenancy category and whether Renters' Rights Act rules apply.
- Check holding deposit, tenancy deposit, rent-in-advance timing, permitted payments, rent amount, and any bidding request against current official guidance.
- For a mainstream private tenancy, current official guidance caps a holding deposit at one week's rent. The tenancy deposit is generally capped at five weeks' rent when annual rent is under £50,000 and six weeks when it is £50,000 or more.
- Right to Rent applies in England only and generally requires checks on adult occupiers before a covered tenancy. Exemptions include some student accommodation and other categories; verify the exact arrangement.
- Check local HMO, selective, or additional licensing with the relevant council. Local schemes vary.
- Check council tax responsibility and any student disregard/exemption with the council.

# Search and Source Strategy

## Source Hierarchy

Use the source that controls or directly observes the claim:

1. government, legislation, council, regulator, university, transport operator;
2. official landlord, developer, building, managing agent, or accommodation operator;
3. major property portal or established letting agent;
4. reputable housing advice or property reporting;
5. forums and social posts only as leads or lived-experience context.

An official building page can support amenities but not current unit availability unless it advertises that unit. A portal can support the advert but not independently verify it.

## Live Search Coverage

Select platforms based on housing type and local coverage. Common starting points include Rightmove, Zoopla, OnTheMarket, OpenRent, SpareRoom, university accommodation pages, PBSA operators, build-to-rent operators, and established local agents.

Do not treat the list as exhaustive or assume every platform covers every nation equally.

For each search:

- use current filters and sort newest when helpful;
- search broad geography and named buildings/streets;
- check direct operator or agent pages for the same unit;
- deduplicate syndicated adverts by address, photos, floor plan, price, and agent;
- record when the advert was added or reduced;
- distinguish available now, available on a stated date, and date unknown.

If normal web access is blocked, use browser interaction when available. Do not bypass login, CAPTCHA, rate limits, or other access controls.

## Current Rules

For legal or policy questions:

- route to the correct UK nation;
- open the current official page at answer time;
- check publication/update and commencement dates;
- distinguish rules in force from announced future rules;
- state material exceptions such as lodgers, halls, social housing, holiday lets, or excluded tenancies.

Use archived or secondary sources only when the current primary source is unavailable, and disclose that limitation.

## Search Completeness

Do not claim “all available homes”. Say which platforms and geographic terms were checked and when.

If the market produces few results:

1. preserve hard constraints;
2. identify the binding constraint;
3. suggest the smallest relaxation;
4. show market comparables separately from genuine matches.
